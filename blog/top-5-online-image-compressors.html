Top 5 Online Image Compressors in 2026

Published on April 14, 2026 • 8 min read

Reducing image file size is crucial for web performance, SEO, and storage management. But with hundreds of tools claiming to be the best, which one should you actually use? We've tested the top 5 online image compressors based on speed, security, and compression quality.

1. LovelyDoc (The Privacy Champion)

While we might be biased, LovelyDoc stands out as the most secure option. Unlike traditional web tools, LovelyDoc works 100% in your browser. Your images are never uploaded to a server, making it the perfect choice for sensitive internal business documents.

2. TinyPNG

A classic in the industry, TinyPNG uses smart lossy compression to reduce the size of your images. It's great for bulk processing of transparent PNGs, though it requires an internet connection for its cloud-side algorithms.

3. Squoosh.app

Developed by Google, Squoosh is an open-source tool that allows you to compare different compression formats (like WebP and AVIF) in real-time. It's a powerful tool for power users who want granular control over every pixel.

4. ImageOptim

If you're on a Mac and prefer a desktop app, ImageOptim is excellent. It strips out unnecessary metadata and applies lossless compression, though its web-based alternatives are catching up fast.

5. Optimizilla

Optimizilla is known for its simple "Before and After" slider, allowing you to see exactly how much quality you are losing at different compression levels. It's a solid, reliable choice for standard JPG compression.
