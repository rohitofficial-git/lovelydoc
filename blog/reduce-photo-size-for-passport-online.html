How to Reduce Photo Size for Passport Applications

Published on April 5, 2026 • 5 min read

Applying for a passport or visa online often comes with a frustrating technical hurdle: the file size limit. Most government portals require your passport photo to be between 20KB and 100KB. If your high-quality smartphone photo is 5MB, you need a reliable way to shrink it without making your face look like a blur of pixels.

Understanding the Requirements

Different countries have different standards, but the most common requirements include:

  • File Size: Usually between 20KB and 100KB.
  • Format: JPEG or JPG is standard.
  • Dimensions: Often 2x2 inches (350x350 pixels or 600x600 pixels).
  • Background: Plain white or off-white.

The Common Mistake: Simple Resizing

Many people try to reduce the size by simply cropping the image or taking a screenshot of the original. This often results in a file that is still too large or has very poor resolution. The key is intelligent compression, which reduces the data weight of the image while preserving the sharp details of your face.

Step-by-Step Guide to 100KB Passport Photos

1. Capture: Take your photo in good lighting against a plain background. Don't worry about the file size yet.

2. Crop: Use any basic photo editor to crop the image so your head is centered and takes up 70-80% of the frame.

3. Compress: Upload your cropped image to an exact KB compressor like LovelyDoc.

4. Set Target: Choose "100KB" or "50KB" depending on your specific application requirements.

5. Download: Save the optimized image and upload it to the government portal.

Why Privacy Matters

When you're dealing with passport photos, you're handling sensitive personal information. You should avoid uploading your face to random servers that process images in the cloud.
