What is PDF Compression?

PDF compression is the process of reducing the file size of a PDF document by optimizing its internal data. This is primarily achieved by compressing high-resolution images, removing unnecessary metadata, and using more efficient data encoding methods. Large PDF files can be a hassle when you need to send them via email or upload them to portals with strict size limits (like 100KB or 200KB). Our tool ensures that you can reduce PDF size while maintaining the highest possible visual quality.

Why Reduce PDF File Size?

There are several reasons why you might need to shrink PDF size:

  • Email Limitations: Most email providers have a 25MB attachment limit. Compressing your PDF ensures it reaches its destination without being bounced back.
  • Faster Uploads: Smaller files upload much faster to websites, school portals, and government applications.
  • Storage Space: If you deal with thousands of documents, reducing file size can save gigabytes of cloud or local storage.
  • Improved User Experience: If you're hosting PDFs on a website, smaller files load significantly faster for your visitors, improving SEO and engagement.
